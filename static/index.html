Why this isn't just RAG

  • Answers come from graph traversal, not single-chunk retrieval. The prescription record never names the patient's condition — it only points to a discharge summary from a different hospital. Vector search over that chunk can't name the condition; the graph walks the link and can.
  • Entities are shared across documents. "Dr. Kulkarni", "the January 2025 admission" and "the rhythm disorder" resolve to the same graph nodes whether they appear in a Pune discharge summary, a Mumbai lab report, or a Chennai prescription.
  • Time is part of the graph. Ingestion runs temporal extraction, so "what changed after she saw Dr. Menon?" is answered by ordering events, not by hoping the right chunk mentions both dates.
